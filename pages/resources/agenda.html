Litterary Theory

It's no coincidence that I am reading this book. In fact, I am studying litterary theory and how it applies within the societal dynamics in the book. For instance, a hegemony is, in essence the societal norms and standards everyon follows, whether you like it or not. That last part is where conflict comes into play. You see, not everyone can fit within said hegemony, they are called the Other. If a character bends and complies, they are a submissive character. However, if they rise to the occasion and stand up for themselves, they are a subversive character.
