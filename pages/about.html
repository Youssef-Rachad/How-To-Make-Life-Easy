About Myself

As a high schooler, I think it's important to introduce
myself before we begin making websites.

Hello, I am Youssef Rachad. I attend École Secondaire Roméo-Dallaire and as part of my English 12 class, I am writing a website on getting organised by making your own HTML creation. I have quite a lot of experience writing code from my programming classes and the enormous free time I spend exploring code. My first big website is this one and it has inspired me to continue developping websites.

You can now see why, when Mrs. Laurin announced that a blog was a possible format for this semester's ISU, I did not hesitate to choose it. This time however, I would like to share this passion of mine with you.



Wether you're a high school student trying to set yourself up for success (I know I am!) or deciding to learn the skills you need to code a website through a concrete application, this blog will be a great oppurtunity for you to learn and experience the joys of getting organised. It doesn't sound very amusing at first, but trust me, this will get interesting very quickly.

Personnally, I am doing this project to better myself in my English class, and also to prepare myself for university, where I hope to study mechatronics engineering. In fact, the spirit of engineering lies in being organised, from planning to scheduling deadlines, and even recording feedback. I will use these various aspects to measure my performance, though I invite you to do the same if you have ongoing projects and would like to stay on top of your tasks.

When I'm not coding, I like to read while drinking tea. Mint tea, green tea, lemon & ginger tea, whith or without honey /milk, you name it! As for my reading material, it has great diversity since I read up on current events and visit old classics, adding on that my bilingualism and the cornucopia of litterary works never ceases to run dry. More specifically, I often read adventure books from H.G. Wells and Jules Vernes because they remind me that life is not about the destination, it's about the journey.

So embark with me as I go exploring far and wide in search of different strategies to get organised and wield the power of HTML at its fullest potential.
